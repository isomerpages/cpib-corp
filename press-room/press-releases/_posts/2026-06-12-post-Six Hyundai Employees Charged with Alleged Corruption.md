<p>On 12 June 2026, six individuals were charged in Court for alleged corruption
offences involving safety-related matters. They are:</p>
<p>a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Shaheen </strong>(32-year-old male Bangladeshi
National), a Safety Coordinator in the employ of Hyundai Engineering &amp;
Construction Co Ltd (“Hyundai”) at the material time of the offences;</p>
<p>b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Siow Hung Wee (“Siow”)</strong> (萧翰玮,
53-year-old male Singaporean), a Safety Manager in the employ of Hyundai
at the material time of the offences;</p>
<p>c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Biswas Uzzal (“Uzzal”)</strong> (42-year-old
male Bangladeshi National), a Safety Coordinator in the employ of Hyundai
at the material time of the offences;</p>
<p>d)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Hossain Mithu Mohammad Arif (“Mithu”)</strong> (33-year-old
male Bangladeshi), a Safety Coordinator in the employ of Hyundai at the
material time of the offences;</p>
<p>e)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Biswas Pradip Chandra (“Pradip”) </strong>(29-year-old
male Bangladeshi), a Safety Supervisor in the employ of Hyundai at the
material time of the offences; and</p>
<p>f)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Uddin Md Mesbah (“Uddin”)</strong> (34-year-old
male Bangladeshi), a Safety Coordinator in the employ of Hyundai at the
material time of the offences.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong><em>Alleged Corruption Offences Involving Shaheen</em></strong>
</p>
<p>2. On multiple occasions, between July 2023 and April 2024, Shaheen allegedly
accepted or attempted to obtain bribes totalling about S$6,200 from safety
coordinators employed by various subcontractors at the worksite located
at 2A Labrador Villa Road, Singapore (the “Site”). The bribes were inducements
for the following:</p>
<p>a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not reporting to his principal unsafe work
acts;</p>
<p>b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not issuing safety administrative charges for
unsafe work acts;</p>
<p>c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Allowing a worker to re-enter the Site to work
after banning him for committing an unsafe work act at the Site;</p>
<p>d)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not banning a worker from the Site after the
worker committed an unsafe work act at the Site; and</p>
<p>e)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Allowing workers to use lifting machinery despite
not being permitted to do so.</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;For his alleged actions, Shaheen faces 15 charges for offences
punishable under Section 6(a) of the <em>Prevention of Corruption Act (“PCA”)</em>,
of which five charges are read with Section 124(8)(a)(ii) of the <em>Criminal Procedure Code (“CPC”)</em>,
and amalgamated under Section 124(4) of the <em>CPC</em>.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong><em>Alleged Corruption Offences Involving Siow, Uzzal, Mithu, Pradip and Uddin</em></strong>
</p>
<p>4.&nbsp;Between 2023 and 2024, over separate occasions, Uzzal, Mithu,
Pradip and Uddin allegedly accepted bribes amounting to about S$2,700 from
safety coordinators employed by various subcontractors. The bribes were
inducements for the following:</p>
<p>a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not reporting to their principal unsafe work
acts;</p>
<p>b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not reporting to their principal future possible
unsafe work acts;</p>
<p>c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not issuing safety administrative charges for
unsafe work acts;</p>
<p>d)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Waiving safety administrative charges for unsafe
work acts;</p>
<p>e)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Allowing workers to commence work at the Site
without attending the required safety course; and</p>
<p>f)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Not banning workers from the Site after they
committed a workplace safety infringement at the Site.</p>
<p>5.&nbsp;Over four occasions between June 2023 and February 2024, Siow
allegedly abetted by engaging in a conspiracy with Uzzal to accept bribes
amounting to S$900 from safety coordinators employed by various subcontractors.
The bribes were an inducement not to issue safety administrative charges.
On two other occasions, within the same period, Siow allegedly conspired
with Mithu to accept bribes amounting to S$1,000 from safety coordinators
employed by various subcontractors. The bribes were inducement to allow
a worker to re-enter the Site to work after banning him for committing
an unsafe work act.</p>
<p>6.&nbsp;On 24 October 2023, Siow allegedly abetted by engaging in a conspiracy
with Uzzal and Mithu to accept a bribe of S$500 from a safety coordinator
employed by a subcontractor. The bribe was an inducement to waive safety
administrative charges for unsafe work acts.</p>
<p>7.&nbsp;Over three occasions between September and December 2023, Uzzal
allegedly abetted by engaging in a conspiracy with Pradip to accept bribes
totalling S$650 from safety coordinators employed by various subcontractors.
The bribes were an inducement to not report future possible unsafe work
acts, not report unsafe work acts, and to allow workers to commence work
at the Site without attending the required safety course.</p>
<p>8.&nbsp;On two occasions in October 2023, Uzzal allegedly abetted by engaging
in a conspiracy with Mithu and Pradip to accept a total of S$700. The bribes
were an inducement to not issue safety administrative charges or not to
report to principal unsafe work acts.</p>
<p>9.&nbsp;For their alleged actions:</p>
<p>a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Siow Hung Wee</strong> faces seven charges
for offences under Section 6(a) read with Section 29(a) of the <em>PCA</em>.</p>
<p>b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Biswas Uzzal</strong> faces 11 charges
for offences under Section 6(a) of the <em>PCA</em>, of which 10 charges
are read with Section 29(a).</p>
<p>c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Hossain Mithu Mohammad Arif</strong> faces
nine charges for offences under Section 6(a) of the <em>PCA</em>, of which
five charges are read with Section 29(a) of the <em>PCA </em>and of which
one charge is amalgamated under Section 124(4) read with Section 124(8)(a)(ii)
of the <em>CPC</em>.</p>
<p>d)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Biswas Pradip Chandra</strong> faces
eight charges for offences under Section 6(a) of the <em>PCA</em>, of which
five charges are read with Section 29(a) of the <em>PCA </em>and of which
one charge is amalgamated under Section 124(4) read with Section 124(8)(a)(ii)
of the <em>CPC</em>.</p>
<p>e)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Uddin Md Mesbah</strong> faces two charges
for offences under Section 6(a) of the <em>PCA</em>, of which one charge
is amalgamated under Section 124(4) read with Section 124(8)(a)(ii) of
the <em>CPC</em>.</p>
<p>10.&nbsp;Singapore adopts a strict zero-tolerance approach towards corruption.
Any person convicted of a corruption offence under Section 6 of the <em>Prevention of Corruption Act </em>can
be fined up to S$100,000 or sentenced to imprisonment not exceeding five
years or both. Any person convicted of an offence amalgamated under Section
124(4) read with Section 124(8)(a)(ii) of the <em>Criminal Procedure Code</em> can
face twice the amount of punishment liable for that offence.</p>

<br>c) Call the Duty Officer at 1800-376-0000; or
<br>5) Write to us at the CPIB Headquarters @ 2 Lengkok Bahru, S159047.</p>
<p>12. Where possible, the report should include the following information:</p>
<p>a) Where, when and how did the alleged corrupt act happen?
<br>b) Who was involved and what were their roles?
<br>c) What was the bribe given and the favour shown?</p>
<p>13. Under the law, the identity of the person making the report is protected,
except where the court finds that the person has intentionally made a false
statement in his or her report. Informers may choose to remain anonymous
when making the report.</p>
